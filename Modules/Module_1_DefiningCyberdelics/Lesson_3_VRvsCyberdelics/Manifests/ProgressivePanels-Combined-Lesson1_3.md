# Progressive Panels Content: Entertainment vs. Cyberdelics: Direction of Attention & Design Dimensions

## Panel: Shifting Intentions: Entertainment vs. Cyberdelic VR

### Section: The Technology is the Same

<p>When you put on a VR headset, the hardware doesn't care if you're playing a zombie shooter or engaging in a deep meditative journey. The pixels are the same. The tracking is the same. The difference isn't in the wires, it's in the way the experience directs your attention. To understand cyberdelics, we have to look past the screen and into the mind.</p>

*Trigger:* Where is for Attention?

---

### Section: Direction of Attention

**Image Location:** ../assets/images/module1/DirectionOfAttention.webp

<p>In <strong>Entertainment VR</strong>, your attention is pulled <strong>outward</strong> toward the content, the score, the enemies, the external goals. In <strong>Cyberdelic VR</strong>, the technology is used to pull your attention <strong>inward</strong> toward your own internal state, breath, or consciousness. The tech is the mirror, not just the window.</p>

*Trigger:* Entertainment Questions

---

### Section: Entertainment Questions

<p>Entertainment VR asks. <em>'What can I do in this world?'</em> or <em>'How do I win?'</em> It focuses on agency within a simulation. The goal is often distraction or thrill, moving away from your current state into a fictional one.</p>

*Trigger:* Cyberdelic Questions

---

### Section: Cyberdelic Questions

<p>Cyberdelic VR asks. <em>'What am I feeling right now?'</em> or <em>'How is my awareness changing?'</em> It focuses on self-observation. The goal is presence, moving toward a deeper understanding of your own consciousness through the technological interface.</p>

*Trigger:* The Different Needs

---

### Section: Different Tools for Different Needs

<p>Neither is 'better.' We need entertainment, play, and escapism. But when we use technology for transformation, we need a different set of design principles. If you try to use a 'game' design to achieve a 'mystical' state, the mechanics often get in the way. Cyberdelics is the art of designing for that inward turn.</p>

## Panel: The Architectural Dimensions of Cyberdelic Design

### Section: The Architecture of Experience

**Image Location:** ../assets/images/module1/ArchitectureOfExperience.webp

<p>Every intentional cyberdelic experience is built across six dimensions. These are not arbitrary checkboxes. They are the fundamental questions a designer must answer to create a coherent intervention. When an experience feels fragmented or ineffective, it is usually because one or more of these dimensions was left undefined.</p>

*Trigger:* The Substrate

---

### Section: Reality and Sensory Channels

**Image Location:** ../assets/images/module1/Substrate.webp

<p>The first two dimensions form the substrate of the experience. Reality is the world the experience inhabits, whether that is a fully digital environment, an augmented physical space, or your own biology. The Sensory dimension defines the perceptual channels used to access that reality. An experience might rely purely on sweeping visual landscapes, or it might center entirely on tactile feedback and acoustic resonance.</p>

*Trigger:* The Internal Shift

---

### Section: Presence and States

**Image Location:** ../assets/images/module1/Internal.webp

<p>The next two dimensions address the internal shift. Presence is the quality of awareness the architecture cultivates, like grounded embodiment or focused mental clarity. States refers to the transient shifts in consciousness that occur during the session. A cyberdelic design might aim to facilitate a temporary ego softening or a profound emotional release, using the reality substrate and sensory channels as the catalyst.</p>

*Trigger:* The Lasting Impact

---

### Section: Traits and Meaning

**Image Location:** ../assets/images/module1/LastingImpact.webp

<p>The final two dimensions define the lasting impact and the soul of the work. Traits are what endure downward into the person's life long after the session ends, such as sustained cognitive flexibility or increased relational empathy. Meaning is the most critical dimension. It encompasses the overarching world, the narrative arc the person moves through, and the specific principle that makes the transformation possible.</p>
