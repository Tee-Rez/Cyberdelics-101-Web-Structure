# External Links & Further Reading

These resources explore the comparative phenomenology and overlapping experiences between chemical psychedelics and cyberdelic technologies.

### Scientific Papers & Research
- [Techno-delics: The Intersection of Technology and Psychedelics](https://www.frontiersin.org/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2022.923989/full) - A paper exploring how technology can simulate, augment, or replace endogenous psychedelic experiences.
- [Comparing the Phenomenology of Virtual Reality and Classic Psychedelics](https://royalsocietypublishing.org/doi/10.1098/rstb.2021.0084) - Examines the subjective overlap between immersive VR and substance-induced states of mind.

### Articles & Essays
- [Can Virtual Reality Be as Potent as Psychedelics?](https://www.wired.com/story/can-virtual-reality-be-as-potent-as-psychedelics/) - A Wired article investigating the therapeutic parallels and user experiences of both modalities.
- [The "Digital Trip": How Tech is Mimicking Acid](https://tim.blog/2021/04/15/tech-psychedelics/) - Discusses the ways sound and light technology seek to trigger endogenous psychedelic responses in the brain.

### Books & Literature
- [How to Change Your Mind by Michael Pollan](https://michaelpollan.com/books/how-to-change-your-mind/) - While focused on psychedelics, this foundational text sets the baseline for the neuroscience of altered states that cyberdelics aim to emulate.

### Projects & Studies
- [The MAPS Podcast: Technology and the Psychedelic Renaissance](https://maps.org/news/podcast/) - Conversations bridging multidisciplinary insights on consciousness expansion and therapy.
