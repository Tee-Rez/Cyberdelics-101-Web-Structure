# External Links & Further Reading

Explore the "Renaissance" era, marked by the concurrent revival of clinical psychedelic research and the maturation of consumer virtual reality technologies.

### Scientific Papers & Research
- [The Psychedelic Renaissance: A New Paradigm for Mental Health (MAPS)](https://maps.org/news/bulletin/the-psychedelic-renaissance-a-new-paradigm/) - An overview of the resurgence in clinical trials for substances like MDMA and psilocybin.
- [The Second Coming of Virtual Reality (IEEE)](https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/8447285) - Discusses the technological breakthroughs (Oculus Rift, HTC Vive) that finally made commercial VR viable in the 2010s.

### Articles & Journalism
- [How Michael Pollan’s Book Sparked the Psychedelic Renaissance](https://www.theatlantic.com/health/archive/2018/05/the-psychedelic-renaissance/560413/) - Examines the mainstream cultural shift regarding altered states of consciousness.
- [The VR Renaissance: From Oculus to the Metaverse](https://techcrunch.com/2019/12/31/a-decade-in-virtual-reality/) - A TechCrunch retrospective on the sudden boom and subsequent steady growth of the VR industry.

### Organizations & Hubs
- [Multidisciplinary Association for Psychedelic Studies (MAPS)](https://maps.org/) - The leading organization pushing for the medicalization of psychedelics during this period.

### Documentaries
- [Fantastic Fungi](https://fantasticfungi.com/) - A visual journey showcasing the mainstreaming of mycology and psychedelics.
- [Print the Legend (Netflix)](https://www.netflix.com/title/80005444) - While focused on 3D printing, it captures the hardware hacker renaissance that paralleled the Oculus VR kickstarter boom.
