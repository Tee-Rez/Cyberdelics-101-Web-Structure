# External Links & Further Reading

Dive into the "Gap Years" of the 1980s and 1990s—the era of the first VR hype cycle, rave culture, and early cyberdelic experimentation.

### Articles & Essays
- [The First VR Boom: A Look Back at the 1990s](https://vator.tv/news/2021-04-09-the-first-vr-boom-a-look-back-at-the-1990s) - Examines the early promises and technological limitations of 90s virtual reality.
- [Rave Culture and the Cybernetic Dream](https://mixmag.net/feature/history-of-rave-culture) - Explores the synthesis of electronic music, rudimentary digital visuals, and altered states that defined 90s warehouse parties.

### Books & Literature
- [Mondo 2000: A User's Guide to the New Edge](https://www.amazon.com/Mondo-2000-Users-Guide-Edge/dp/0060969288) - The quintessential magazine and book capturing the radical techno-optimism and cyberdelic philosophy of the early 90s.
- [Cyberia: Life in the Trenches of Hyperspace by Douglas Rushkoff](https://rushkoff.com/books/cyberia/) - Explores the cultural intersection of cyberpunk, the early internet, and the psychedelic revival.

### Multimedia & Presentations
- [Mindmachines and the Early Neuro-technology Movement](https://hackaday.com/2016/01/29/retrotechtacular-the-mind-machines-of-the-1980s/) - A look back at the light-and-sound brainwave entrainment devices (like the Dreamachine) popularized during this era.
- [Terence McKenna on Virtual Reality (YouTube Archive)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Fq2Iu_h3g48) - Archival audio of the famous psychonaut predicting VR's potential as a tool for manifesting the imagination.
