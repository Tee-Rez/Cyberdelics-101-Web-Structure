# External Links & Further Reading

Explore the early pioneers who bridged the emerging worlds of computing, cybernetics, and consciousness expansion during the 1960s and 70s.

### Historical Articles & Essays
- [We Owe It All To The Hippies](https://time.com/archive/6597405/we-owe-it-all-to-the-hippies/) - A classic Stewart Brand article from TIME detailing how counterculture fed into the PC revolution.
- [What the Dormouse Said: How the Sixties Counterculture Shaped the Personal Computer Industry by John Markoff](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/296727/what-the-dormouse-said-by-john-markoff/) - Essential book detailing the intertwined history of psychedelics and Silicon Valley.

### Biographies & Foundational Texts
- [Timothy Leary’s Cyberpunk Philosophy](https://boingboing.net/2015/10/22/timothy-learys-cyberpunk-phi.html) - Discusses Leary’s later thesis that "the PC is the LSD of the 1990s."
- [The Center of the Cyclone: An Autobiography of Inner Space by John C. Lilly](https://www.amazon.com/Center-Cyclone-Autobiography-Inner-Space/dp/0978933649) - Chronicles the sensory deprivation tank experiments that predate modern immersive cyberdelics.

### Archival Media & Projects
- [The Whole Earth Catalog Archive](https://wholeearth.info/) - Explore the original publications that married ecological awareness, counterculture, and early tools for personal computing.
- [From Counterculture to Cyberculture by Fred Turner](https://press.uchicago.edu/ucp/books/book/chicago/F/bo3774005.html) - Academic deep dive into how the Whole Earth network laid the groundwork for modern digital utopianism.
