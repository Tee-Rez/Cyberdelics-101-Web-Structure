# External Links & Further Reading

These resources explore the leading scientific frameworks for understanding consciousness and how technological interventions interact with these models.

### Scientific Papers & Research
- [Integrated Information Theory: From Consciousness to Its Physical Substrate](https://www.nature.com/articles/nrn.2016.44) - A foundational paper by Giulio Tononi on IIT, a leading theory on consciousness metrics.
- [The Global Workspace Theory of Consciousness](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/11283745/) - Bernard Baars's seminal work detailing how consciousness acts as a global broadcaster for brain processes.

### Articles & Essays
- [What is Consciousness? (Scientific American)](https://www.scientificamerican.com/article/what-is-consciousness/) - An accessible overview of the "hard problem" of consciousness and current neurological approaches.
- [Altered States and the Default Mode Network](https://www.theatlantic.com/health/archive/2015/06/the-science-of-psychedelics/396655/) - Explores how dampening the DMN—whether via chemicals or technology—leads to states of ego dissolution.

### Books & Literature
- [Consciousness Explained by Daniel C. Dennett](https://www.amazon.com/Consciousness-Explained-Daniel-C-Dennett/dp/0316180661) - A cognitive science classic proposing a "multiple drafts" model of human consciousness.
- [The Feeling of What Happens by Antonio Damasio](https://www.amazon.com/Feeling-What-Happens-Emotion-Consciousness/dp/0156010755) - Explores the somatic markers and emotional underpinnings of our conscious experiences.

### Videos & Lectures
- [Anil Seth: Your Brain Hallucinates your Conscious Reality (TED Talk)](https://www.ted.com/talks/anil_seth_your_brain_hallucinates_your_conscious_reality) - A phenomenal presentation on predictive coding and how digital inputs (like VR) can seamlessly alter our perceived reality.
- [David Chalmers: How Do You Explain Consciousness? (TED Talk)](https://www.ted.com/talks/david_chalmers_how_do_you_explain_consciousness) - Discusses the "Hard Problem" of consciousness.
