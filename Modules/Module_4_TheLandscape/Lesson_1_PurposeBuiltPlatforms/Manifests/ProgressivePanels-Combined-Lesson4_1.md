# Progressive Panels Content: Purpose-Built Platforms: Mapping the Cyberdelic Application Landscape

## Panel: Mapping the Landscape

### Section: Meditation & Mindfulness

**Image Location:** ../assets/images/module4/meditation.webp

<p>The first category is Meditation & Mindfulness - the wellness foundation of the cyberdelic landscape. These are platforms designed for daily practice, stress reduction, focus training, and building a consistent contemplative routine. Think of this as the VR equivalent of meditation apps like Headspace or Calm, but with the added dimension of full immersion.</p><ul><li><strong>Who it serves:</strong> People seeking consistent wellness practices, beginners to meditation, anyone managing everyday stress.</li><li><strong>Key characteristics:</strong> Accessible entry points, no clinical diagnosis required, designed for solo use, usually subscription-based.</li></ul>

### Section: Therapeutic & Clinical

**Image Location:** ../assets/images/module4/clinical.webp

<p>The second category is Therapeutic & Clinical - evidence-based VR interventions for diagnosable conditions. These aren't wellness tools. They're medical-grade applications used by licensed healthcare providers to treat specific disorders like PTSD, phobias, chronic pain, and anxiety disorders.</p><ul><li><strong>Who it serves:</strong> Patients working with therapists, psychologists, pain specialists, or Licensed clinicians. Not for self-directed use.</li><li><strong>Key characteristics:</strong> Clinical validation required, used within healthcare settings, often covered by insurance, targets specific diagnoses.</li></ul>

*Trigger:* Next Category

---

### Section: Biofeedback-Enhanced

**Image Location:** ../assets/images/module4/biofeedback.webp

<p>The third category is Biofeedback-Enhanced - platforms that create a real-time loop between your body's signals and the virtual environment. Your heart rate, brainwaves, breathing pattern, or stress response literally shapes what you see and hear in VR. The experience responds to you, and you respond in turn.</p><ul><li><strong>Who it serves:</strong> People training specific physiological states (calm, focus), biohackers, clinical populations learning self-regulation.</li><li><strong>Key characteristics:</strong> Requires sensors (EEG, heart rate), higher cost barrier, steeper learning curve, uniquely cyberdelic feedback loops.</li></ul>

*Trigger:* Next Category

---

### Section: Exploratory & Transformational

**Image Location:** ../assets/images/module4/transformational.webp

<p>The fourth category is Exploratory & Transformational - experiences designed for deep consciousness work, mystical states, ego dissolution, simulated psychedelic journeys, and profound perspective shifts. This is the far end of the spectrum where the goal isn't stress reduction or symptom relief. It's transformation, insight, awe, and altered states.</p><ul><li><strong>Who it serves:</strong> Experienced consciousness explorers, people in deep personal development work, facilitators-in-training.</li><li><strong>Key characteristics:</strong> High intensity, not for beginners without guidance, often facilitated, requires readiness screening and integration.</li></ul>

### Section: The Spectrum & Choosing Your Category

**Image Location:** ../assets/images/module4/spectrum.webp

<p>These four categories exist on a spectrum - not as rigid boxes, but as a continuum. Many platforms span multiple categories. What matters is knowing where an experience sits on this map so you can match it to the right person.</p><p><strong>Choosing Your Category - Key Questions</strong></p><ul><li><strong>Goal?</strong> (Stress vs. Treatment vs. Training vs. Insight)</li><li><strong>Comfort Level?</strong> (Beginner vs. Experienced)</li><li><strong>Support Needed?</strong> (Solo vs. Professional Oversight)</li><li><strong>Experience Level?</strong> (Foundation vs. Depth Work)</li></ul>

## Panel: Two Philosophies

### Section: TRIPP: The Engagement Philosophy

**Image Location:** ../assets/images/module4/tripp.webp

<p>TRIPP operates on the belief that traditional mindfulness can feel too slow or vague for modern minds. Its answer is <strong>wellness gamified</strong>. Every session gives feedback, tracks streaks, adapts to your mood, and rewards consistency.</p><ul><li><strong>Founded:</strong> 2017 by Nanea Reeves</li><li><strong>Key Feature:</strong> Visual progress tracking to combat 'meditation boredom'</li><li><strong>Platforms:</strong> Quest, PSVR2, Pico (~$15/mo)</li></ul>

*Trigger:* See TRIPP Modes

---

### Section: TRIPP: The Four Modes

<p>TRIPP organizes experiences by intended state</p><ul><li><strong>CALM:</strong> Stress reduction, gentle visuals (5-10 min)</li><li><strong>FOCUS:</strong> Attention training, dynamic environments (10-15 min)</li><li><strong>SLEEP:</strong> Wind-down protocols, dimming visuals (15-30 min)</li><li><strong>DEEP:</strong> Extended altered states, intense visuals (20-40 min) - touches the 'Exploratory' boundary</li></ul><p>The platform adapts based on mood reports and optional biometric data.</p>

*Trigger:* Gamification & Evidence

---

### Section: TRIPP: Gamification & Evidence

<p>TRIPP tracks streaks, minutes practiced, and mood shifts. Research backs this approach. a 2021 study with OHSU showed significant anxiety reduction from a single session. It also integrates with sensors (Apple Watch, Muse) for biofeedback, bridging the Wellness and Biofeedback categories.</p>

*Trigger:* FloatVR

### Section: FloatVR: The Fascination Philosophy

**Image Location:** ../assets/images/module4/floatvr.webp

<p>FloatVR was built on a specific insight from Attention Restoration Theory. directed concentration is exhausting, and most people already have too little of it. The way to restore the mind isn't to demand more effort from it but to give it something genuinely fascinating to drift through without strain.</p><p>Every FloatVR session drops you into a procedurally generated world that is different each time you visit. There are no meditation instructions, no breathing cues you're required to follow, no correct way to use it. You float through shifting, dreamlike environments accompanied by original music composed specifically for the experience.</p><ul><li><strong>Platform:</strong> Meta Quest</li><li><strong>Designed for:</strong> Anxiety relief, focus recovery, sleep preparation, and general mental renewal</li><li><strong>Notable:</strong> Built with particular attention to ADHD users who struggle with the concentration demands traditional meditation places on them</li></ul>

*Trigger:* FloatVR: Who It Serves Best

---

### Section: FloatVR: Who It Serves Best

<p>FloatVR's generative design means no two sessions are alike, which solves a problem that static environments can't. habituation. People stop returning to apps when the experience becomes predictable. FloatVR's worlds regenerate each visit, so there's always something new to notice and follow.</p><p>The platform appeals most to people who already know how to let go but want an environment that rewards that capacity.</p><ul><li><strong>Experienced meditators</strong> who find traditional apps too structured</li><li><strong>People with ADHD</strong> who find eyes-closed practices difficult</li><li><strong>High cognitive load professionals</strong> who need to release without adding more tasks to the recovery process</li></ul><p>The trade-off is the same one that defines FloatVR's whole philosophy. there is no structure, no instruction, and no guidance. Beginners who need to be taught how to meditate will not find that here. FloatVR assumes you are ready to receive an experience, not be guided through one.</p>

*Trigger:* TRIPP vs. FloatVR

---

### Section: TRIPP vs. FloatVR: When to Recommend Each

<p>Which do you recommend? It depends entirely on who you're talking to.</p><p><strong>Recommend TRIPP when the person.</strong></p><ul><li>Is new to meditation and needs structure</li><li>Responds well to progress tracking and visible milestones</li><li>Wants biometric integration or mood logging</li><li>Values polished production and session variety</li><li>Is willing to pay $15/month for a platform that actively supports their consistency</li></ul><p><strong>Recommend FloatVR when the person.</strong></p><ul><li>Is comfortable letting go without being guided</li><li>Finds gamification and tracking more distracting than motivating</li><li>Struggles with the concentration demands of traditional meditation (including ADHD or high cognitive fatigue)</li><li>Wants something genuinely new each session without requiring effort to access it</li></ul><p>Both are legitimate approaches to wellness VR. As a facilitator, the sophisticated recommendation is. 'Based on what you've told me about what motivates you and how you relate to structured practice, here's which one I think will actually work for you, and here's why.'</p>

## Panel: Enosis Therapeutics & Emerging Platforms

### Section: Enosis Therapeutics: VR as a Therapy Adjunct

**Image Location:** ../assets/images/module4/enosis.webp

<p>Enosis Therapeutics was founded by Australian researchers Agnieszka Sekula and Dr. Prash Puspanathan around a single observation. psychedelic experiences are embodied, emotional, and largely ineffable, but the therapy frameworks we use to integrate them are predominantly cognitive and verbal. Talk therapy asks someone to put into words something that resisted language while it was happening. That's a fundamental mismatch, and it's where insights get lost.</p><p>Enosis builds VR scenarios designed to bridge that gap. Their tools are not standalone wellness platforms, they're clinical adjuncts. software deployed at specific points in a psychedelic-assisted therapy protocol, always alongside a licensed provider, always in the context of a substance session.</p><ul><li><strong>First study:</strong> VR combined with psychedelics, conducted with Swinburne University</li><li><strong>First clinical deployment:</strong> Partnership with OVID Clinics in Berlin and the MIND Foundation</li></ul>

*Trigger:* SurrenderVR & AnchoringVR

---

### Section: Surrender VR and Anchoring VR: The Two Core Tools

<p>Enosis has developed several VR scenarios deployed at different points in the therapeutic arc. Two are central to understanding their approach.</p><p>SurrenderVR is used on dose day, shortly after a patient has taken their psychedelic but before the full effects have arrived. It's a calming 15-to-20-minute meditative environment designed to reduce pre-peak anxiety and help the patient soften into the experience rather than resist it. The goal is not to direct the trip but to lower the psychological friction at the moment when letting go matters most. Critically, the headset comes off before the psychedelic experience peaks. Enosis is explicit that VR is not used during the active altered state.</p><p>AnchoringVR is used four to five hours later, as the experience begins to resolve. The patient enters a beachside VR environment and is presented with floating jewel-like objects. Holding each one, they speak aloud. recording an insight, a feeling, or an image that emerged during the session. Their own voice is attached to that object. Across subsequent integration sessions, these recorded insights can be revisited, they begin connecting to each other visually, and patients re-encounter the emotional quality of their experience in a way that talk therapy alone rarely achieves.</p>

*Trigger:* How Enosis Changes Integration

---

### Section: How Enosis Changes Integration

<p>What makes AnchoringVR different from journaling or talking with a therapist is the multisensory quality of re-entry. When a patient returns to that beach environment in a later session and touches the object tied to a particular insight, they don't just recall the thought. they re-access something closer to the felt quality of the experience itself. Participants in Enosis's early studies reported being surprised by how much they remembered when prompted this way, and by how emotionally alive those memories remained.</p><p>Across multiple integration sessions, the insights grow and connect. Seeds planted on the beach get larger. Separate realizations merge into broader patterns. The patient's own words, recorded in the immediate aftermath of one of the most significant experiences of their life, become the primary material of integration. not the therapist's interpretation of what happened, but the patient's direct expression of it.</p><p>Enosis is not a consumer product. Practitioners encounter it through clinical partnerships, research collaborations, and the training pathways emerging around psychedelic-assisted therapy as the field develops regulatory frameworks. Its significance for this course is what it demonstrates about VR's potential at the clinical edge of the cyberdelic spectrum. precise, evidence-informed, and fundamentally oriented toward outcomes that no solo wellness platform could achieve.</p>

### Section: Flowly: Biofeedback for Chronic Conditions

<p>Flowly sits between clinical and consumer worlds. It targets chronic pain and anxiety using real-time biofeedback to teach nervous system regulation.</p><ul><li><strong>Mechanism:</strong> VR environment expands/contracts based on heart rate/breathing (requires sensor like Muse).</li><li><strong>Status:</strong> Used in some pain clinics; subscription-based consumer access.</li><li><strong>Key Takeaway:</strong> Without sensors, it's just visuals. With sensors, it's a training tool.</li></ul>

### Section: Innerworld: Peer-Support Social VR

**Image Location:** ../assets/images/module4/innerworld.webp

<p>Innerworld flips the model. Guides are community members with lived experience, not professionals. It offers group spaces for grief, anxiety, and recovery.</p><ul><li><strong>Model:</strong> Peer-led social VR (acquired by XRHealth in 2023).</li><li><strong>Pros:</strong> Authentic connection, accessible/low-cost.</li><li><strong>Cons:</strong> Variable quality, not for acute crisis.</li><li><strong>Question:</strong> Where is the line between peer support and professional care?</li></ul>

### Section: Three Models, Three Trade-offs

<p>You now have three distinct platform models in view.</p><ul><li><strong>TRIPP and FloatVR (Solo Wellness):</strong> User-directed, affordable subscriptions, accessible entry, build daily practice. Trade-off: No human support, user must self-motivate, less depth than what clinical contexts can provide.</li><li><strong>Enosis Therapeutics (Clinical Adjunct):</strong> Precision VR tools deployed inside licensed psychedelic-assisted therapy, evidence-informed, designed for insight anchoring and integration. Trade-off: Requires a clinical provider and a substance session, this is not something a facilitator offers independently.</li><li><strong>Innerworld (Peer Community):</strong> Free or low-cost, authentic connection, lived experience, fills the gap between wellness apps and professional therapy. Trade-off: Variable quality, not appropriate for acute crisis, relies on volunteer sustainability.</li></ul><p>No single model is best. The sophisticated facilitator knows all three exist, can articulate who each serves, and understands which one requires a referral rather than a direct recommendation.</p>
