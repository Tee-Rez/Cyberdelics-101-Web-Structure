Right now, we make it explicit so it becomes second nature.</p>

*Trigger:* Step 1: Identify Goal

---

### Section: Step 1 - Identify Goal

<p>The first question is always. <strong>what outcome is this person seeking?</strong> Not 'what do they think they want' - what result would actually serve them?</p><ul><li><strong>Daily meditation/mindfulness -></strong> Float VR or TRIPP Calm</li><li><strong>Therapeutic work (PTSD, phobias, clinical anxiety) -></strong> Psious/Limbix/Oxford VR + licensed professional</li><li><strong>Pain management -></strong> RelieVRx or XRHealth (clinical), Flowly (biofeedback self-directed)</li><li><strong>Deep transformation/mystical experience -></strong> Enosis with facilitator, or Isness-D if research access available</li><li><strong>Breathwork practice -></strong> Othership (community), or integrate into other platforms</li><li><strong>Music/sound journey -></strong> Soundself (technodelic), Wavepaths (therapeutic music)</li><li><strong>Fitness/flow state -></strong> Supernatural or FitXR with contemplative intent</li><li><strong>Creativity as practice -></strong> Open Brush</li><li><strong>Social/community connection -></strong> VRChat (open), Sacred VR (vetted), Innerworld (peer mental health)</li><li><strong>Experimental/high-intensity -></strong> Assess readiness carefully, then Enosis or experimental platforms with preparation</li></ul><p><strong>The nuance.</strong> Sometimes the stated goal and the actual need differ. Someone saying 'I want a death simulation' might actually need grief therapy. Someone asking for 'the most intense VR experience' might actually need to start with basic meditation. Your job is discerning the underlying need, not just matching stated requests to platforms.</p>

*Trigger:* Step 2: Consider User

---

### Section: Step 2 - Consider User

<p>Once you know the goal, assess <strong>who this person is</strong> and what they bring.</p><p><strong>Experience Level.</strong></p><ul><li>Beginner to consciousness work -> Start accessible (Float VR, TRIPP), build foundation before intensity</li><li>Experienced meditator/contemplative -> Can handle depth immediately (Enosis, Isness-D, experimental)</li><li>Psychedelic background -> May seek intensity but still needs VR-specific orientation</li></ul><p><strong>Clinical Vulnerability.</strong></p><ul><li>Stable, no acute conditions -> Full range available</li><li>Active mental health challenges -> Clinical platforms with professional oversight required</li><li>Trauma history -> Preparation and facilitation non-negotiable for high-intensity work</li><li>Contraindications (psychosis, severe dissociation, acute crisis) -> Refer to professional care, not VR</li></ul><p><strong>Tech Comfort.</strong></p><ul><li>VR-savvy -> Can navigate complex platforms (Resonite, VRChat)</li><li>Basic tech comfort -> Mainstream platforms (Quest ecosystem apps)</li><li>Tech-anxious -> Need hands-on setup support, simplest interfaces (Float VR free tier)</li></ul><p><strong>Budget.</strong></p><ul><li>Limited/fixed income -> Free tiers (Float VR), affordable options (Nature Treks $10 one-time), community support (Innerworld)</li><li>Moderate budget -> Standard subscriptions ($10-20/month)</li><li>Can invest -> Premium platforms (Supernatural $20/month), facilitated sessions (Enosis $150-300)</li></ul><p><strong>Values/Privacy.</strong></p><ul><li>No concerns about data collection -> Quest ecosystem is optimal</li><li>Privacy-conscious -> Pico hardware, open-source platforms where possible</li><li>Specific ethical constraints -> Honor them, find alternatives</li></ul><p><strong>The principle.</strong> Matching platform to person requires seeing the whole person, not just the presenting request.</p>

*Trigger:* Step 3: Match Context

---

### Section: Step 3 - Match Context

<p>Where and how will this practice happen? Context shapes what's viable.</p><p><strong>Setting.</strong></p><ul><li>Home solo practice -> Standalone platforms, self-directed content</li><li>Clinical setting -> Professional platforms (Psious, Limbix), therapist-administered</li><li>Group/community -> Social VR (Sacred VR, Innerworld), or synchronized solo (Othership groups)</li></ul><p><strong>Support Structures Available.</strong></p><ul><li>Has therapist -> Can integrate clinical VR as adjunct to therapy</li><li>No professional support -> Wellness/growth platforms only, refer for clinical needs</li><li>Has facilitator access (you or others) -> Can engage deeper work with appropriate container</li><li>Isolated, no support -> Start gentle, build capacity, avoid high-intensity without backup</li></ul><p><strong>Facilitator Required vs. Appropriate.</strong></p><ul><li>Some platforms require facilitation (Enosis - not designed for solo)</li><li>Some benefit from facilitation but work solo (TRIPP, Float VR - better with guidance but functional alone)</li><li>Some are explicitly self-directed (Nature Treks, Open Brush)</li></ul><p><strong>Physical Space.</strong></p><ul><li>Small apartment -> Seated/stationary experiences work, movement VR may not</li><li>Dedicated VR space -> Full range including active movement</li><li>Institutional setting -> Consider noise, privacy, multiple users</li></ul><p><strong>Frequency/Duration.</strong></p><ul><li>Daily short practice -> Low-friction platforms, easy setup</li><li>Weekly deep sessions -> Can tolerate more setup complexity for higher quality</li><li>One-time/occasional -> Guided facilitated sessions may be better than self-directed subscription</li></ul><p><strong>The reality check.</strong> The perfect platform that doesn't fit someone's actual life circumstances won't get used. Recommendations must be practically viable.</p>

*Trigger:* Step 4: Trial & Iterate

---

### Section: Step 4 - Trial & Iterate

<p>The selection framework isn't 'choose once perfectly.' It's 'start well, then refine based on experience'.</p><ul><li><strong>Use Free Trials (7-14 days typical):</strong> Most platforms offer trials. Use them. Don't commit to annual subscriptions before testing.</li><li><strong>Try 2-3 Options When Unclear:</strong> If you're genuinely torn between TRIPP and Float VR, try both. The trial period reveals which resonates.</li><li><strong>Give Each Platform 3-5 Sessions:</strong> One session isn't enough to know if it works. Platforms have learning curves, states vary day-to-day. Commit to at least 3-5 sessions before judging.</li><li><strong>Reassess at 30 Days:</strong> After a month: Is this serving you? Are you actually using it? Is the outcome matching the intention? If yes, continue. If no, adjust.</li><li><strong>Permission to Change:</strong> What serves you at one stage may not serve you at the next. Beginners outgrow beginner platforms. Clinical needs shift as healing progresses. Context changes. The right answer now isn't the right answer forever.</li></ul><p><strong>The meta-skill.</strong> The framework itself becomes invisible with practice. You'll stop consciously running through 'Step 1, Step 2...' and start intuitively matching person to platform. But building that intuition requires starting with the explicit framework.</p>
